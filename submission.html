Submission Materials

What to Prepare & Submit

Pitch Deck

  • Format: PDF or PowerPoint (max 20 slides).
  • Language: English only.
  • Content: Problem statement, proposed quantum solution, methodology, expected outcomes, team bios.
  • Visuals: Diagrams, circuit designs, and flowcharts are encouraged.

Technical Report

  • Format: PDF, max 15 pages (excluding references).
  • Structure: Abstract, Introduction, Methodology, Results, Discussion, Conclusion.
  • Citations: IEEE or APA format required.
  • Equations: LaTeX formatting preferred for quantum expressions.
Optional

Prototype / Demo

  • Format: Link to GitHub repository, simulation, or video demo (max 5 min).
  • Code: Must be original; third-party libraries must be declared.
  • Simulation using Qiskit, Cirq, or similar frameworks is accepted.
Optional

Posterboard

  • Size: A0 portrait (841mm × 1189mm).
  • File: High-resolution PDF submitted digitally; physical print at Finals.
  • Summarizes project visually for the Olympiad Exhibition.
  • Required for in-person Finals participation.

Evaluation Criteria

How Projects are Scored

EXP Stream

Scientific Rigor30%
"Is the research methodology sound and reproducible?"
  • Validity of quantum formalism and notation
  • Quality of citations and prior art review
  • Reproducibility of proposed experiments
Innovation35%
"Does this advance the state-of-the-art?"
  • Novelty of research approach
  • Potential impact on QCIT field
  • Differentiation from existing literature
Presentation35%
"Is the work communicated clearly?"
  • Clarity of pitch deck and report
  • Quality of posterboard design
  • Oral presentation at Finals

APP Stream

Technical Depth30%
"Is the quantum application technically sound?"
  • Correctness of quantum algorithms used
  • Feasibility of the proposed implementation
  • Handling of real-world constraints
Impact & Relevance35%
"Does this solve a meaningful real-world problem?"
  • Clarity of problem definition
  • Scalability of the proposed solution
  • Alignment with industry or societal needs
Presentation35%
"Is the application well communicated?"
  • Clarity of pitch and report
  • Quality of demo/prototype (if provided)
  • Effectiveness at Finals exhibition
